📋 Student Code of Conduct & Trip Rules

All students and chaperones are expected to follow WorldStrides and Merryhill School guidelines throughout the trip. Violations may result in parents being called and students being sent home at family expense.

1.Supervision at all times. Students must stay with their assigned group and Course Leader at all times. No wandering away from the group without explicit permission from a chaperone or Course Leader.
2.Curfew. All students must be in their hotel rooms by the designated curfew each night. Doors must remain closed. No hallway socializing after lights-out.
3.Room rules. No students of different genders in each other's rooms at any time. No food or beverages that could damage hotel property.
4.Electronics. Phones are permitted for photos and communication with parents. They must be silenced during tours, memorials, theater performances, and any instructional time. Sharing of location with parents is encouraged.
5.Dress code. Appropriate clothing at all times. Closed-toe shoes recommended for all walking days. A nicer outfit is required for the Broadway theater evening (May 15).
6.Conduct at memorials & museums. Respectful, quiet behavior is mandatory at Arlington National Cemetery, Holocaust Memorial Museum, 9/11 Memorial & Museum, and all war memorials. No photos in restricted areas. Follow all posted rules.
7.Purchases & money. Students are responsible for their own spending money beyond allotted amounts. Teachers/chaperones will not hold student money. Keep wallets secure in busy areas like Times Square.
8.Bags & security. Minimize bags for Statue of Liberty (airport-style security) and Mt. Vernon (no backpacks/packages allowed in mansion). All bags stay on coach during Arlington Cemetery visit.
9.Medications. All prescription medications must be disclosed to chaperones before departure. Students may not share medications. Over-the-counter medications must be pre-approved by parents and declared to trip leaders.
10.Lost or separated. If separated from the group, stay calm and in place. Contact a nearby adult in uniform (security, museum staff) and call your Course Leader or WorldStrides emergency line immediately: 1-800-468-5899.
11.Social media. Students may post trip photos, but must not post real-time location updates, hotel names, or room numbers on public social media during the trip for safety reasons.
12.Illness. Report any illness, injury, or feeling unwell immediately to a chaperone. Do not try to hide being sick. The group carries a basic first aid kit; serious medical issues will be escalated to emergency services.

🚨 Emergency Procedures

Medical Emergency

1. Call 911 immediately for any life-threatening situation.

2. Notify the nearest Course Leader or chaperone at once.

3. Do not move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger.

4. WorldStrides 24/7 Emergency: 1-800-468-5899

5. Stay with the student — do not leave them alone.

Lost / Missing Student

1. Immediately alert all nearby chaperones and Course Leaders.

2. Last known location, time, and what the student was wearing — gather this info fast.

3. Do not allow the rest of the group to disperse searching alone.

4. Contact venue security; in NYC, contact NYPD non-emergency: (646) 610-5000.

5. Contact WorldStrides: 1-800-468-5899

6. Notify parents only after WorldStrides and school administration have been contacted.

If a Student Feels Unsafe or Encounters a Threat

1. Move away from the threat calmly. Alert a Course Leader immediately.

2. In a public emergency (evacuation, lockdown): follow all instructions from law enforcement and venue staff.

3. Text is often faster than calls in crowded emergency situations — text Course Leader and parents.

4. Pre-designated group meetup point: The nearest hotel lobby, or Grand Central Terminal main concourse (NYC) / Hyatt Bethesda main entrance (D.C.)

Severe Weather

Course Leaders monitor weather forecasts daily. In the event of severe weather (thunderstorm, tornado watch), all outdoor activities will be suspended and the group will move to the nearest indoor facility. Follow Course Leader instructions without delay.
